Türkiye Air Quality Results

Analysis of Zonal Mean and Maximum Values of NO₂, PM10, and PM2.5 (2013–2022)

The three bar charts illustrate the annual trends of the zonal mean (in orange) and maximum (in red) values for the main atmospheric pollutants: nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), coarse particulate matter (PM10), and fine particulate matter (PM2.5), across Turkey from 2013 to 2022.

NO₂(Nitrogen Dioxide)

• The maximum NO₂ values remain relatively stable between 2013 and 2015, with a sharp peak in 2016, followed by a gradual decline and a subsequent rise in 2020–2021.

• The mean values are consistently low (between 2 and 4), with minor fluctuations, particularly in 2016 and 2021.

• The substantial gap between mean and maximum values suggests the presence of localized or temporary pollution peaks.

PM₂.₅(Particulate Matter ≤2.5 µm)

• Maximum PM2.5 concentrations rise sharply from 2018, peaking in 2020. After a slight decrease in 2021, values increase again in 2022.

• Mean values remain relatively stable but are slightly higher compared to the 2013–2016 period.

• As with PM10, the significant difference between mean and maximum values in recent years indicates episodes of high local concentrations.

PM₁₀(Particulate Matter ≤10 µm)

• Maximum PM10 levels show a significant upward trend starting from 2018, peaking in 2022

This indicates a worsening of air quality in the most recent years.

• The mean values also increase, though more gradually and with less variability.

• From 2019 onwards, the widening gap between mean and maximum values points to potential short-term pollution events or regional disparities.

Conclusions

The data show a general deterioration in air quality in Turkey starting from 2018, particularly for particulate matter (PM10 and PM2.5). While the mean annual concentrations have increased moderately, the maximum values have risen sharply, pointing to acute pollution episodes.

NO₂ values are more stable, though peaks in certain years highlight fluctuating pollution levels. These findings underscore the need for more effective environmental policies to mitigate air pollution.

Spatial overview of air pollution

The three maps provide a comprehensive spatial overview of air pollution in Turkey, focusing on different pollutants.

NO₂ Map

This map focuses on nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) levels in 2020, showing pronounced concentrations around major urban and industrial zones, particularly in the northwest, including the Istanbul region, and along the western coast.

Elevated NO₂ levels are strongly associated with high traffic density and industrial emissions. Central and eastern parts of the country display much lower concentrations, suggesting less urban influence and lower emission sources in these areas.

PM₂.₅ Map

This map illustrates fine particulate matter (PM₂.₅) concentrations across Turkish districts. The spatial pattern shows generally moderate values in central and northern regions, while higher concentrations appear scattered in parts of western and southeastern Turkey.

These elevated levels may be linked to urbanization, industrial activities, and local topographical or meteorological conditions that can trap pollutants.

PM₁₀ Map

The map displays PM₁₀ levels for 2020, revealing a more widespread distribution of higher values compared to PM₂.₅. Significant hotspots are visible in western coastal areas, parts of central Anatolia, and the southern regions.

The broader spread of PM₁₀ could reflect the contribution of natural sources like dust, in addition to anthropogenic emissions from traffic, construction, and industrial processes.

The maps highlight the spatial heterogeneity of air pollution in Turkey, emphasizing the impact of urbanization, industrial activities, and geography on air quality. Such insights are essential for policymakers aiming to target interventions in the most affected regions.
