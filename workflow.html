Project Workflow Overview

Step-by-step data processing and geospatial analysis pipeline used in the Türkiye Air Quality study.

Turkey

Turkey is a geographically diverse country located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia. The majority of its territory lies in Asia (Anatolia), while a smaller portion extends into Europe (Eastern Thrace). It is bordered by eight countries and surrounded by three major bodies of water: the Aegean Sea to the west, the Mediterranean Sea to the south, and the Black Sea to the north.

The country's landscape includes a variety of geographical features such as mountains, coastal plains, inland plateaus, and river valleys. This diversity contributes to a range of climatic zones — from the warm, dry Mediterranean climate along the coast to the more continental climate in the interior regions.

Turkey’s environmental challenges include air and water pollution, deforestation, and vulnerability to natural hazards such as earthquakes and droughts. Its strategic position also makes it significant for regional water management, energy transport, and environmental monitoring.

Major urban centers include Ankara, the capital, and Istanbul, the largest city, which lies on both sides of the Bosporus Strait — a key waterway connecting the Black Sea to the Mediterranean.

Types of data we have used:

• CAMS Europe Air Quality Reanalyses

a high-resolution dataset developed by the Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service.

The platform visualizes hourly concentrations of key pollutants such as PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, O₃, and CO, with a spatial resolution of approximately 10 km. The data is available at multiple vertical levels, allowing users to assess pollution not only at the surface but also through different layers of the atmosphere.

By integrating validated and near-real-time satellite-modelled data, this tool supports: • Identifying pollution hotspots • Analyzing seasonal and long-term trends • Exploring vertical pollution profiles • Supporting informed decision-making in public health and environmental planning

• Copernicus Satellite Land Cover Dataset

This dataset delivers global annual land cover maps from 1992 to the present, with a spatial resolution of 300 meters. It is based on satellite observations and classifies land cover into 22 categories following the international FAO Land Cover Classification System (LCCS).

• WorldPop

provides robust, high-resolution demographic data that fills critical gaps in traditional census sources—especially in underserved regions. Its global gridded population outputs, with detailed age and gender compositions, are used to inform public health strategies, disaster response, urban development, and policymaking. Its commitment to transparency, collaborations, and open-access tools makes it a cornerstone in spatial demography and global development mapping.

Step 1 Data Collection

This project focuses on the spatial and temporal analysis of air pollutants in Turkey. We used data from the CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis service, specifically targeting three major pollutants:

nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), fine particulate matter (PM2.5), and coarse particulate matter (PM10). The datasets, in NetCDF format, cover the period from January 2013 to December 2022 .

Step 2 Pollutant maps (NO2, PM2.5, PM10)

for the month of December 2022

Collective data were imported into QGIS as mesh layers, reprojected to EPSG:4326 (WGS 84), and processed using the Mesh Calculator to obtain monthly average concentrations for December 2022. The outputs were then converted to GeoTIFF raster layers using the Rasterize Mesh Dataset tool .

Step 2 Turkey 2022 Average pollutant maps (NO2, PM2.5, PM10)

To analyze long-term trends, we aggregated the monthly rasters into annual averages using the r.series tool in QGIS. We created average pollutant maps for each year, with a focus on the year 2022.

Step 5 Turkey Reclassified ESA CCI Land Cover

We retrieved the ESA CCI Land Cover 2022 dataset from the Copernicus Climate Data Store. After importing it into QGIS and reprojecting it, we simplified the original 22-class legend into 11 generalized categories based on the IPCC classification scheme using the Reclassify by Table tool .

Step 6 Turkey zonal statistics vector layer

To ensure alignment, we resampled the CAMS pollutant rasters to match the land cover resolution. This enabled us to perform zonal statistics to calculate mean and maximum pollutant values per land cover type (urban) for the years 2013 to 2022 .

Step 7 Population Exposure and Bivariate Mapping

We incorporated 2020 population data from WorldPop, which provides gridded population estimates. The dataset was reclassified into five quantile-based population density classes using the r.quantiles tool, allowing for more meaningful exposure analysis .

To assess combined pollution and exposure, we used FAO GAUL Level 2 administrative boundaries and performed zonal statistics for both population and pollutant concentration maps. This allowed us to generate bivariate maps showing the intersection of high/low pollution with high/low population density, represented via a 5x5 color matrix .

Step 8 Exposure Summary with Pie Charts

Finally, we dissolved the bivariate layers by pollution class and calculated the total population per class using zonal statistics. These results were visualized with pie charts, showing the proportion of the population exposed to different pollution levels, providing a clear overview of public health risk by pollutant type .
